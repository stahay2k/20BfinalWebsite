Description/Objectives of Mission

Led by NASA's Goddard Space Flight center in Maryland. Predicted launch is in May 2027. Field of view of NGR space telescope is 100 times larger than Hubble Telescope. Combined with extremely precise and high-resolution camera, the Nancy Grace Roman space telescope will image our galaxy, measure and describe our galaxy's structure, and survey the birth of stars, explore interaction of smaller galaxies with ours.

  • Explore Dark Energy and find answer to questions we don't know about
  • Similar to EUCLID mission by ESA
  • Use 3 main techniques: Baryon acoustic oscillations, observation of distant supernovae, weak gravitation lensing
  • Answer questions about the existence of life on exoplanets

My Thoughts on Nancy Grace Roman Space Telescope

NGR telescope is set to launch by May 2027. Considering that there are still a few years to go, there is a lot that this telescope can do. There is huge potential for exciting discoveries in regards to exoplanets and discovering life on such planets. In my opinion, NGR will compliment the James Webb telescope recently sent into space. By 2027, we will have some images from James Webb and this mission can expand upon those discoveries. Something that I found really interesting throughout Physics 20B was Dark Matter and Dark Energy. NGR will focus on these and we might find something simply shocking and unimaginable. I am also excited to see how much more we can learn about our growing universe through this powerful piece of technology.
